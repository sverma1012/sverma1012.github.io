Sneha Verma

Teaching English

Tutor

Location: Residence in India. Children came from the colony behind my residence.

I tutored underprivileged children in English during my gap year. Through the duration of teaching them, I learned the necessity and significance of humbleness. IN a country like India, where the financial gap between people can be huge, it is important to respect others irrespective of their economic status. The children, despite not being provided opportunities to receive a good-quality education, were hardworking, determined, and talented.

The honor of being their teacher gave me immense pleasure and joy. I started teachign them English from the basics, i.e. alphabets and numbers, and continued to build up on that foundation. Their school teachers commended their progress and gave them more homework and tasks for improvement.

As a tutor, my responsibility was to guide the students in learning a new language and embrace it despite their hesitation and lack of experience. Through this experience, I havlearned the importance of hardwork and determination in achieving a goal in the face of obstacles.
